Eid ul-Fitr:

Eid al-Fitr, also known as the Festival of Breaking the Fast, marks the end of Ramadan, the Islamic holy month of fasting. Muslims celebrate with special prayers, feasting, giving of charity (zakat al-fitr), and exchanging greetings and gifts with family and friends.

Eid al-Adha:

Eid al-Adha, also known as the Feast of Sacrifice, commemorates the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) to sacrifice his son as an act of obedience to God. Muslims worldwide celebrate by attending prayers, sacrificing animals, sharing meat with the needy, and gathering for festive meals.

Mawlid al-Nabi:

Mawlid al-Nabi celebrates the birth of the Prophet Muhammad, the founder of Islam. While the observance varies among different Islamic communities, it often includes recitations of poetry and religious hymns, gatherings to learn about the life and teachings of the Prophet, and charitable acts.
