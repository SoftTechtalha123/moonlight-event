Diwali:

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most widely celebrated festivals in India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. People illuminate their homes with oil lamps, burst fireworks, exchange sweets, and seek the blessings of deities.

Holi:

Holi, the Festival of Colors, is a vibrant and joyous celebration that marks the arrival of spring. Participants engage in playful color fights, dance to traditional music, and indulge in festive delicacies. It promotes unity, harmony, and the spirit of brotherhood.

Navratri:

Navratri is a nine-night Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. It is celebrated with great fervor and devotion across India, particularly in the state of Gujarat. People engage in traditional dance forms like Garba and Dandiya Raas, adorned in colorful attire, as a form of worship and celebration.
