2.Calgary International Children's Festival (Late May)

The Calgary International Children's Festival is an annual event held in late May in Calgary, Alberta, Canada. The festival was first held in 1987 and has since grown to become one of the largest arts festivals for young audiences in North America.

3.Vesak (Date varies)

Vesak, also known as Buddha Day, is a major Buddhist festival that commemorates the birth, enlightenment, and death of Gautama Buddha, the founder of Buddhism. The festival is celebrated on the full moon day in the month of Vesakha (usually in May, but the date varies depending on the lunar calendar) and is observed by Buddhists around the world.

The festival is celebrated with various activities, including visiting temples, offering alms to monks, chanting of Buddhist scriptures, and lighting lanterns. Buddhists also perform acts of generosity, such as giving food and clothing to the needy, as a way of honoring the Buddha's teachings on compassion and kindness.

Vesak is not only a time of celebration but also a time for Buddhists to reflect on the teachings of the Buddha and to strive for personal spiritual development. It is a day of joy and gratitude for the wisdom and compassion that the Buddha is believed to have brought to the world.

4.Gawai Dayak (May 31) :

Gawai Dayak is a festival celebrated in the Malaysian state of Sarawak on the island of Borneo, primarily by the Dayak people, who are the indigenous people of Sarawak. The festival is a time of thanksgiving and marks the end of the rice harvesting season.

The origins of Gawai Dayak can be traced back to the early 1960s when a group of Dayak intellectuals led by Michael Buma developed the idea of a festival to celebrate the Dayak culture and way of life. They wanted to create an event that would bring the Dayak people together to celebrate their cultural identity and traditions.

The first Gawai Dayak festival was held on June 1, 1964, and since then, it has become an annual event celebrated on May 31st and June 1st. The festival typically begins with a ceremony called the "Miring" ceremony, where offerings are made to the gods and spirits to ensure a successful harvest and to thank them for their blessings.

During Gawai Dayak, traditional rituals and ceremonies are performed, including the "Ngabang" or visiting ceremony, where families and friends visit each other to strengthen bonds and share food and drink. There are also cultural performances, such as traditional music and dance, as well as games and competitions.

Gawai Dayak is an important cultural event for the Dayak people, as it allows them to showcase their traditions and customs to the wider community. It is also a time for the Dayak people to come together to celebrate their unity and to honor their ancestors and the land that sustains them.

5. Flores de Mayo (May)

Flores de Mayo, also known as "Flowers of May," is a festival celebrated in the Philippines in honor of the Virgin Mary. The festival has its roots in the Catholic traditions brought by Spanish colonizers to the Philippines in the 16th century.

The festival is typically held during the entire month of May, which is considered the "Month of Mary" in Catholic tradition. It is a time when Filipinos pay homage to the Virgin Mary through prayers, processions, and offerings of flowers. The festival also often includes the recitation of the rosary, a series of prayers dedicated to Mary.

One of the highlights of Flores de Mayo is the Santacruzan, a religious-historical pageant that reenacts the finding of the True Cross by Queen Helena, the mother of Emperor Constantine. The Santacruzan features a procession of young women and men dressed in elaborate costumes, representing biblical characters and historical figures associated with the Christian faith.

Over the years, Flores de Mayo has become a popular cultural and religious festival in the Philippines, celebrated not only in churches but also in schools, communities, and even in Filipino communities abroad. It is a colorful and festive event that reflects the strong devotion of the Filipino people to the Virgin Mary and their rich cultural heritage.
